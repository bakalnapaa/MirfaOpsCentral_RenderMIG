📖 Logistics — User Guide
How to use the Logistics section
📋 Record Material Movement

Use this to record any movement of equipment or materials between sites. Each submission generates a downloadable Material Gatepass PDF and logs the record in Logistics History.

1
Open the form — Click 📋 Record Material Movement on the Logistics Home page.
2
Site From / Site To — Enter where the materials are being moved from and where they are going. Example: MIRFA DC → MADINAT ZAYED DC.
3
Date — Defaults to today's date. Change it if needed.
4
Material Status — Select Outward if materials are leaving the site, or Inward if they are arriving.
5
Add Items — The form starts with 3 item rows. Fill in each item's Serial No., Description, Logs, Destination, and QTY. Click ＋ Add Item to add more rows as needed. Remove unused rows with the ✕ button.
6
ZT DC OPS Representative — Your name is pre-filled from your account. Designation is fixed as Data Center Technician. Enter your contact number.
7
Driver & Vehicle Details — Enter the driver's name, vehicle plate number, and contact number.
8
For Security Approval — Enter the security officer's name. Date is auto-classified as today.
9
Submit & Download — Click ✓ Submit & Download PDF. The pass is saved to the database and the Material Gatepass PDF is downloaded automatically to your device.
💡 Pass Numbering: Each pass is automatically assigned a unique number in the format MP-YYYYMMDD-NNN (e.g. MP-20260307-001). Numbers reset and increment per day.
⚠️ Important: Double-check all serial numbers and quantities before submitting. Once saved, the record cannot be deleted — only viewed and re-downloaded.
📦 Logistics History

A full log of all material movement passes recorded. Click any row to view the complete details of that pass.

1
View all passes — The Logistics History table shows every recorded pass with its pass number, date, direction, item summary, total count, destination, and who logged it.
2
View pass details — Click any row in the table to open the full detail view — all items, signatory information, and movement details for that pass.
3
Re-download PDF — Inside the pass detail view, click ⬇ Re-download PDF to regenerate and download the gatepass PDF for that pass at any time.
📋 Columns explained: Pass No. — unique ID for the pass. Direction — Inward (arriving) or Outward (leaving). Items — a summary of what was moved. Count — total quantity across all items.
📖 User Guide
Operations reference — how to use each feature
⚙️ Adding Activities

Use this to log any maintenance or troubleshooting action performed on a miner. Both Admin and User can log activities freely.

1
Open the form — Click the ⚙️ Record Activity button on the Submer Ops Home page.
2
Select the Activity Type — Choose the type that best matches what was done. See the reference table below for descriptions.
3
Fill in the details — Enter the miner IP address, diagnosis of the issue, remarks or actions taken, and the technician who performed the work.
4
Submit — Click Submit. The activity will be logged under your shift and visible in Activities History immediately.
Activity Type Reference
Activity TypeWhen to use it
Miner RebootAny activity involving rebooting or restarting a miner.
Offline TroubleshootingFuse replacements, burnt cable replacements, or miners going offline.
CB ReplacementControl board replacement on any Antminer or Avalon unit.
Power Cable ReplacementPreventive maintenance — replacing burnt power cables.
4-pin Replace/ReplugAny activity where a 4-pin connector was found faulty and replaced or replugged.
6-pin Replace/ReplugAny activity where a 6-pin connector was found faulty and replaced or replugged.
Hashboard TighteningLoose screws or bus bars were found and retightened.
Health Check - Sleep/WakeMiner triggered the health check protocol and was put to sleep or woken up.
💡 Tip: Be as specific as possible in the Diagnosis and Remarks fields. This data is used in shift reports and serial history tracking.
⛏️ Replacing Miners

Use this to record a miner unit swap — when a faulty miner is pulled out and a new one is installed in its place.

1
Open the form — Click ⛏️ Replace Miner on the Submer Ops Home page.
2
Enter the Location — Specify the IP address of the slot where the replacement is happening. Make sure this is correct.
3
New Serial Number — Enter the serial number of the replacement unit being installed. Double-check before submitting.
4
Old Serial Number — Enter the serial number of the unit being removed. The system will automatically look up its current record.
5
Remarks / Notes — Log the reason for replacement and any observations about the faulty unit. This will be saved to the old serial's record.
6
Submit — Click Submit All. You can add multiple replacements in one submission using ＋ Add Another.
📦 After submission: The old miner's location is automatically set to WH1 and the remarks are saved to its record. The new miner is marked as Onboarded at the specified location.
🔋 Replacing PSUs

Use this to record a PSU swap — when a faulty PSU is removed and replaced with a working unit.

1
Open the form — Click 🔋 Replace PSU on the Submer Ops Home page.
2
PSU Model — Select the specific model used (GPW121215 or APW121215).
3
Location — Enter the IP address of the miner slot where this PSU is installed. Specify this correctly.
4
New Serial Number — Enter the serial of the replacement PSU being installed.
5
Old Serial Number — Enter the serial of the PSU being removed.
6
Old PSU Status — Select the new status for the removed PSU (Faulty, For Testing, etc.).
7
Remarks / Notes — Log the fault details and any observations. This will be saved to the old PSU's record.
8
Submit — Click Submit All.
⚠️ Avalon PSUs: The Replace PSU form is for Antminer PSUs only. For Avalon PSU status updates, use the 🔧 Equipment Update option instead.
📦 After submission: The old PSU's location is automatically set to WH1 and the remarks are saved to its record. The new PSU is marked as Tested at the specified location.
🔧 Equipment Update

Use this when a specific miner or PSU needs a status update — without doing a full replacement. This is useful when a unit comes back from repair, is declared unrepairable, or changes state.

1
Open the form — Click 🔧 Equipment Update on the Submer Ops Home page.
2
Equipment Type — Select whether you are updating a Miner or a PSU.
3
Update Type — Miner — First select the Miner Type: Antminer or Avalon.
If Antminer:
Consolidation — Miner has been fully consolidated. Bad — Miner confirmed as bad after assessment. Repaired — Miner has come back from repair and is functional. Unrepairable — Miner has been declared beyond repair.
If Avalon:
For Repair — Miner is faulty and needs repair. Repaired — Miner has returned from repair. In Repair — Miner is currently being repaired.
4
Update Type — PSU — First select the PSU Type: Antminer or Avalon.
If Antminer:
Tested — PSU has been tested and is working. For Testing — PSU requires testing / validation. Faulty — PSU has been confirmed faulty.
If Avalon:
New Spare — Brand new PSU, not yet deployed. Operational — PSU is working and attached to a miner. You will be asked for the Miner Serial it is attached to. Repaired — PSU has returned from repair. For Repair — PSU is faulty and needs to be sent for repair. Sent for Repair — PSU has been sent to the repair hub. Scrapped — PSU has not returned or is beyond repair / out of warranty.
5
Serial Number — Enter the serial number of the unit to be updated. Make sure this is entered correctly.
6
Submit — Click Confirm Update. The change is logged to the unit's serial history automatically.
💡 Note: Equipment Update does not replace a unit — it only changes the status of an existing record. Use the Replacement forms if you are physically swapping units.
🔍 Checking Serial History

Every miner and PSU has a full change timeline — every replacement, status update, and edit is recorded and viewable by serial number.

1
Open any serial — In Submer tables (Replacement History, Consolidation History, Equipment DB), click a serial number.
2
Serial History popout opens — The timeline appears as a popout on the same page.
3
View the record — Current details appear: model, location, status, remarks, and who last updated it.
4
Change Timeline — All logged events are shown in chronological order: replacements, updates, imports, and manual edits, including date/time and operator.
💡 Tip: You can look up both the new and old serial in a replacement. Each serial has its own independent history — so the old unit shows when it was removed and why, and the new unit shows when it was installed.
📋 Activities History

A full log of all activities recorded across all shifts. Use the filters to find specific entries quickly.

1
Go to Activities History — Click ⚙️ Activities History in the Submer Ops navigation menu.
2
Search — Use the search bar to filter by activity type, IP address, diagnosis, or technician name.
3
Filter by Type — Use the Activity Type dropdown to show only a specific category (e.g. only CB Replacements).
4
Filter by Date — Use the date picker to view activities from a specific day.
5
Filter by Status — Filter by the activity resolution status (Open, Resolved, Monitoring, etc.).
6
Edit an activity — You can edit activities that belong to your current active shift today only. Previous shifts are read-only. Admins can edit any activity.
7
Real-time updates — The table updates automatically when any user logs a new activity. No need to refresh the page.
💡 Tip: The Recent Activities card on the Submer Ops Home also shows the latest 20 entries at a glance and updates in real-time.
🔌 Remote Actions

Use Remote Actions to quickly open the web interface of a PDU or miner directly from the platform without manually typing IP addresses.

Access PDU
1
Click 🔌 Access PDU in the Remote Actions card on Submer Ops Home.
2
Enter Container and Tank — Container: 1–50, Tank: 1–12.
3
IP Preview — The resolved IP address (10.Container.Tank.20) is shown live as you type. Confirm it looks correct before proceeding.
4
Click Go → — The PDU interface opens in a new browser tab.
Access Miners
1
Click ⛏️ Access Miners in the Remote Actions card on Submer Ops Home.
2
Enter Container, Tank, and Miner Position — Container: 1–50, Tank: 1–12, Miner Position: 1–16.
3
IP Preview — The resolved IP address (10.Container.Tank.MinerPosition) updates live as you type.
4
Click Go → — The miner interface opens in a new browser tab.
💡 Tip: The IP preview updates in real time — always confirm the address before clicking Go to avoid opening the wrong device.
📤 Inventory-X Upload

The Inventory-X Upload feature keeps your external Inventory-X system in sync with the MIRFA Operations Platform. Whenever miners are replaced or their status changes, they are automatically flagged as pending upload. This guide shows you how to generate and submit the upload file.

Step-by-Step: Uploading to Inventory-X
1
Click 📤 Upload to Inventory-X in the Actions card on the Submer Ops Home page. The platform will scan the database and display all miners currently pending update.
2
Review the pending list. The modal shows a scrollable preview of all serials to be uploaded, formatted exactly as Inventory-X expects:
Asic Miner / <Serial> / <Model> / <Location> / <Status>
3
Click ⬇️ Download Excel — A .xls file is generated and downloaded immediately. Each row contains one formatted entry ready to paste or import into Inventory-X.
4
Upload the file to Inventory-X. Open the downloaded file and perform the update in your Inventory-X system as normal.
5
Click ✅ Updated? once the upload is complete. This marks all included serials as updated in the database and logs the action in Activities History automatically.
